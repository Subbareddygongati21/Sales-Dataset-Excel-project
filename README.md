# 📊 Sales Performance Dashboard Using Microsoft Excel

## Project Overview

The Sales Performance Dashboard is an interactive Business Intelligence and Data Analysis project developed using Microsoft Excel. The purpose of this project is to transform raw sales transaction data into meaningful business insights that help organizations monitor performance, evaluate profitability, and make data-driven decisions.

Businesses generate thousands of sales records every year, making it difficult to manually track revenue, profits, product performance, customer behavior, and market trends. This dashboard addresses that challenge by consolidating sales information into a single interactive reporting solution. Users can quickly analyze business performance across multiple dimensions such as countries, products, business segments, months, quarters, and years.

The dashboard utilizes Excel's advanced analytical features including Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers, KPI Cards, and Dynamic Visualizations to provide a comprehensive overview of organizational performance. Decision-makers can instantly filter data and gain insights without manually analyzing large datasets.

---

## Business Problem

Organizations often struggle to answer important business questions such as:

* Which products generate the highest profit?
* Which countries contribute the most revenue?
* Are actual sales meeting budget expectations?
* Which business segments perform best?
* How do monthly sales trends compare against targets?
* What areas require improvement to increase profitability?

Without a centralized reporting system, analyzing these questions can be time-consuming and inefficient. This dashboard was developed to provide quick answers through interactive visual reporting.

## Dashboard Development Process

The dashboard was developed through a structured process consisting of several stages.

### Data Preparation

The raw sales dataset was first cleaned and organized to ensure consistency and accuracy. Date fields were standardized, duplicate records were removed, and calculated metrics were verified.

### Data Analysis Using Pivot Tables

Multiple Pivot Tables were created to summarize and aggregate the sales data. These Pivot Tables form the analytical foundation of the dashboard and allow efficient calculation of key business metrics.

The Pivot Tables were used to analyze:

* Revenue by Segment
* Revenue by Country
* Revenue by Product
* Profit by Segment
* Profit by Country
* Profit by Product
* Units Sold by Segment
* Units Sold by Country
* Units Sold by Product
* Monthly Revenue Trends
* Budget vs Actual Performance

### Dashboard Design

After creating the Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts were developed to visualize the data. KPI cards were added to highlight important business metrics. Slicers were implemented to provide interactivity, allowing users to filter data by year, quarter, month, and segment.

The final dashboard was designed to present business insights in a clean, user-friendly, and executive-level reporting format.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The dashboard tracks four major performance indicators that help measure business success.

### Total Revenue

Total Revenue represents the total value generated from sales after accounting for discounts.

Actual Revenue: £213,983,614

Budget Revenue: £210,138,410

Performance Against Budget: +1.8%

The company exceeded its revenue target, indicating strong sales performance and successful market penetration.

### Total Profit

Total Profit measures the earnings generated after deducting operational and product-related costs.

Actual Profit: £56,429,310

Budget Profit: £60,429,310

Performance Against Budget: -6.6%

Although revenue exceeded expectations, profit remained below target, suggesting increased costs or lower-margin product sales.

### Profit Margin

Profit Margin measures the percentage of revenue retained as profit.

Actual Margin: 26.37%

Budget Margin: 28.76%

Performance Against Budget: -8.3%

This indicates that profitability efficiency was lower than expected.

### Units Sold

Units Sold measures the total quantity of products sold during the reporting period.

Actual Units Sold: 1,350,956

Budget Units Sold: 1,420,956

Performance Against Budget: -4.9%

The organization sold fewer units than planned despite generating higher revenue.

## Revenue Analysis

The Revenue Analysis section provides a comprehensive view of how sales are distributed across different countries, products, and business segments.

The dashboard reveals that Government and Small Business segments contribute the highest revenue. Country-wise analysis shows that Japan is the strongest-performing market, generating the highest revenue among all countries. Monthly trend analysis allows users to identify seasonal patterns and monitor business growth throughout the year.

This information helps management allocate resources more effectively and focus on high-performing markets.

---

## Profit Analysis

Profit analysis focuses on identifying which products, segments, and regions contribute most to organizational profitability.

The dashboard highlights that City Cruiser is the most profitable product, contributing nearly half of total profit. In contrast, Trail Master and Yellow Edition generate negative profit, indicating potential pricing, cost, or demand issues.

By identifying profitable and unprofitable products, management can make informed decisions regarding product strategy, pricing optimization, and inventory planning.
